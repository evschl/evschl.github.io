CRAP Website Evaluations

GOOD SITE: Quo

Quo is a finance app startup from Charlotte with a very well put-together site.

This site is visually appealing and readable in many ways, the most immediately noticeable of which being the color. Its background is a deep blue and text is white, which contrast so much that Coolors rates it a 19.29, far above the 8 for it to be acceptable. It's main color, which it most associates with its brand, is a lighter violet. This also contrasts very well, and most images have a low-opacity violet filter over them, making them more easy to look at. Everything is also large, with rounded and easy-to-read font. Lower on the page the alignment is immaculate, placing everything within perfect distance of one another and maintaining consistency. The same fonts and colors are used throughout, utilizing repetition effectively. Proximity is also near perfect, as every section of the webpage is clearly divided from the others.



BAD SITE: Yale's Art Page

This might be low-hanging fruit, as this website was probably intentionally designed to be visually unappealing, but it does almost perfectly represent what a serious website, one whose intention is to present itself sincerely, should definitely not do.

This site is intended to be a sort of virtual bulletin-board, where students and faculty will post upcoming events and news relevant to the school of art at Yale. Let's start with the good: The individual posts are clearly delineated from one another with a solid black line with consistent spacing, and the contents of the posts often display quite a lot of artistic prowess.
However, that's about where the good ends. Its font is completely inconsistently sized, even in elements that are supposedly grouped by proximity. Related is how awfully proximity is implemented. There is a long "quick links" dropdown that moves the entirety of the rest of the page down to be able to fit it. Just two boxes use this awful white-to-yellow gradient. The links section is snugly nestled NEXT to the title of the page, not above or below it. The on-this-page element is so poorly aligned that even though the dropdown would not overlap if it were placed higher, it is still moved down, and to put the cherry on top is hidden in a complete visual soup. There seems to be no consistent color, as we see orange, yellow, black, green, white, light blue, neon pink, lime, and dark blue on the same page. The background somehow manages to have low-resolution and high-resolution areas, looking like this image was corrupted before it was placed on the site.
