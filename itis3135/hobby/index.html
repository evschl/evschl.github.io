Starting Out

This page will be about an ongoing, on-and-off project I've dedicated myself to doing. Well, not exactly, since I haven't kept up with it for a good half a year, but if I were to have any amount of desire to play the Pokémon series, I would continue in this long, arduous journey.

Even if you've never touched a piece of Pokémon media in your life, it is still the largest video game franchise in the world, so it's unavoidable that you at least know of its existence. I'm sure you've at least heard the phrase “Gotta Catch 'Em All!” stapled to every piece of marketing material ever produced for the series. As ubiquitous as that phrase is in the context of Pokémon, actually CATCHING them all is quite a difficult task that very few people ever actually accomplish (without the use of cheating devices). This is especially difficult considering that each game is cumulative. This means that for, say, the 5th game in the series, you would have to catch all of the Pokémon introduced in the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, AND 5th games. Still using the 5th game example, this means that you would have to catch a grand total of 649 Pokémon, each with their own individual requirements.

Really, this was an excuse to play the game on the right, which I had owned for a while. This is the 3rd wave of games released, referred to as “Generation 3”. Luckily, unlike the later games, you can get all of the Pokémon in this generation without having to transfer from previous games. To complete this task, I'll need to catch 386 Pokémon.
